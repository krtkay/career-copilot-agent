# LinkedIn and Professional Networking

## How do I make my LinkedIn profile stand out?
Start with a professional photo and a headline that states your role and value, not just
a job title, using keywords recruiters search for. Write an "About" section in the first
person that summarizes your strengths, experience, and what you are looking for, mirroring
the language of your target roles. Fill in experience with accomplishment-focused bullets
like a resume, list relevant skills, and keep everything consistent with your resume.
Recruiters search LinkedIn with keyword filters much like an ATS, so relevant terms in
your headline, About, and skills sections improve how often you appear.

## How do recruiters find candidates on LinkedIn?
Recruiters use LinkedIn's search and filtering to find candidates by title, skills,
location, and keywords, so profiles that use standard, in-demand terms surface more
often. Setting your profile to signal you are open to work (privately to recruiters if
you prefer) and keeping your location and desired titles accurate helps you appear in the
right searches. Engagement and a complete profile also improve visibility.

## How do I network effectively online?
Build relationships before you need them. Connect with people you have genuinely
interacted with, and when sending a request to someone new, add a short personal note
explaining why. Engage thoughtfully with others' posts, share useful content in your
field, and reach out for brief informational conversations rather than immediately asking
for a job. The goal is a real professional relationship, so lead with curiosity and
generosity, and follow up to keep connections warm.

## How do I ask for a referral or informational chat?
Keep the message short, specific, and easy to say yes to. Remind the person how you know
them or why you are reaching out, state briefly what you are looking for, and make one
clear request, such as a fifteen-minute call or a referral for a specific role. For a
referral, attach your tailored resume and the job link so it is effortless for them.
Always thank them, and follow up afterward with the outcome, which strengthens the
relationship for the future.

## Should I engage with content and build a presence?
Yes, modestly. You do not need to become an influencer, but posting or thoughtfully
commenting on topics in your field makes you more visible to your network and to
recruiters, and demonstrates genuine interest and expertise. Share what you are learning,
comment on industry developments, and celebrate others' work. A consistent, professional
presence compounds over time and keeps you top of mind when opportunities arise.
