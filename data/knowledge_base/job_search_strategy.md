# Job Search Strategy

## What is the most effective way to find a job?
Applying to online postings is only one channel, and on its own it is one of the least
effective, because popular postings attract hundreds of applicants. The strongest
results come from combining channels: applying to well-matched roles, but also
networking and seeking referrals, since a large share of roles are filled through
connections and referred candidates are far more likely to be interviewed and hired.
Treat networking as central, not optional, and treat online applications as one input
rather than the whole strategy.

## How many jobs should I apply to, and how?
Favor quality over volume. A smaller number of carefully targeted, tailored applications
to roles you genuinely fit will outperform mass-applying to everything. For each role,
tailor your resume and, where appropriate, your cover letter to the posting. Track your
applications so you can follow up and learn what is working. Blasting the same generic
resume at hundreds of jobs feels productive but converts poorly.

## How do I use referrals and networking?
Before applying to a company, check whether you know anyone there or have a second-degree
connection who could refer you, because an employee referral often fast-tracks your
application past initial screening. Reach out with a short, specific message. Beyond
direct referrals, informational conversations with people in roles or companies you are
targeting build relationships and surface unadvertised openings. Give as well as ask:
offer help, share useful things, and stay in touch rather than only appearing when you
need something.

## How should I handle employment gaps or a career change?
Be honest and frame gaps around what you did or learned, such as caregiving, study,
freelancing, or volunteering, rather than hiding them. For a career change, lead with
transferable skills and a professional summary that explains your direction, and use
the cover letter to tell the story your resume cannot. Relevant projects, courses, or
freelance work can bridge a gap and show current capability. Confidence and a clear
narrative matter more than an unbroken timeline.

## How do I stay organized and motivated during a job search?
Track every application, its status, and next steps in a simple tracker so nothing slips
and you can follow up on time. Set a sustainable routine rather than sporadic bursts,
and define weekly process goals you control (applications tailored, people contacted)
rather than only outcome goals you do not. Job searching is emotionally draining and
rejection is normal, so protect your energy, ask for support, and treat each interview
as practice that compounds.
