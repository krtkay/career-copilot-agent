# Salary Negotiation

## Should I negotiate my salary?
Yes, in most cases. Employers usually expect some negotiation and often make a first
offer with room built in, so accepting immediately can leave money on the table. The
first salary of a role also compounds over a career through percentage raises and future
offers benchmarked to it, which makes a successful negotiation early especially valuable.
Negotiate professionally and in good faith; done respectfully, it rarely rescinds an
offer and often earns respect.

## How do I research my market value?
Before discussing numbers, establish a realistic range for the role, your experience
level, and your location, since pay varies widely by geography and industry. Use salary
data sources, published ranges in job postings, and market salary tools, and factor in
the cost of living for the location. Talk to people in similar roles if you can. Aim to
walk in with a defensible target range grounded in data rather than a single number, and
know your minimum acceptable figure privately.

## When should I talk about salary?
Delay specific numbers until you have an offer or the employer raises it, because your
leverage is highest once they have decided they want you. If asked early for your salary
expectations, you can give a researched range, deflect politely by saying you would like
to learn more about the role first, or turn the question around and ask their budgeted
range for the position. Avoid being the first to name an exact figure when you can
reasonably avoid it.

## How do I actually negotiate an offer?
When the offer arrives, thank them and ask for time to consider it rather than accepting
or countering on the spot. Then counter with a specific, justified number, ideally
slightly above your target so there is room to settle, and anchor it to your market
research and the value you bring. Keep the tone collaborative, not adversarial, framing
it as finding a package that works for both sides. Get the final agreed offer in writing
before you resign from a current role.

## What can I negotiate besides base salary?
Total compensation is more than base pay. If the employer cannot move on salary, you can
often negotiate a signing bonus, more equity, a performance-review-and-raise timeline,
extra paid time off, remote or flexible-work arrangements, a professional-development
budget, a better job title, or relocation assistance. Decide in advance which of these
matter most to you so you can trade flexibly. Evaluate any offer on the whole package,
not base salary alone.
