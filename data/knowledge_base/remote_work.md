# Remote and Hybrid Job Searching

## How do I find remote jobs?
Search explicitly for "remote", "work from home", "distributed", or "hybrid" in job titles
and filters, and use the remote filters on job boards. Be aware that "remote" can mean
fully remote, remote within a country or region, or hybrid with some office days, so read
the location terms carefully and confirm the expectation. Networking still applies:
referrals help for remote roles too, and some of the best remote openings spread through
communities and networks in your field.

## How do I tailor my resume and profile for remote roles?
Signal that you can thrive without in-person supervision. Highlight experience working
independently, managing your own time, and collaborating across locations or time zones,
and name the remote-collaboration tools you know (video conferencing, chat, shared docs,
project trackers). Strong written communication, self-direction, and reliability are what
remote employers screen for, so make evidence of those visible in your bullets and summary.

## How are remote interviews different?
Remote interviews are conducted by video, so the video-interview basics matter even more:
test your equipment and connection, choose a quiet, well-lit space with a tidy background,
look at the camera, and have a backup plan if the connection drops. Expect questions about
how you stay productive and communicate while remote, how you handle time zones, and how
you collaborate asynchronously. Have specific examples ready.

## What should I ask about a remote role?
Clarify whether it is fully remote or hybrid and how many office days are expected, which
time zones the team spans and whether there are core overlap hours, how the team
communicates and stays connected, and whether there are location restrictions for legal or
tax reasons. Also ask how the company supports remote workers, for example equipment
stipends or home-office budgets. These answers reveal whether the arrangement actually fits
your life.

## How do I stay productive and visible while working remotely?
Set a consistent routine and a dedicated workspace, communicate proactively about progress
and blockers, and make your work visible since managers cannot see it in a hallway. Use
shared docs and trackers so your contributions are documented, respond reliably, and build
relationships intentionally through video calls and chat. Over-communicating slightly is
better than going quiet, especially early in a remote role.
